### 2. Panel B: The Spectral Abscissa Profile ($\lambda_{\text{max}}$)

This monitors the linear stability margin of the dominant atmospheric mode.

* **What it plots:** Maximum Real Eigenvalue $\max_i \text{Re}(\lambda_i)$ vs. $\gamma$.
* **Visual Structure:** The line trends upward toward the zero-axis as $\gamma$ drops. Because it is a Hopf bifurcation, $\lambda_{\text{max}}$ crosses the critical $Y = 0.0$ threshold with a non-zero imaginary component (signaling the frequency of the emerging oscillation).

### 3. Panel C: Minimum Eigenvalue Distance ($d_{\text{min}}$)

This tracks eigenvalue crowding and overall system stiffness near the transition zone.

If there were multiple secondary modes crowding the axis (mode interaction), you would see a flattening or clustering of lines here. Instead, it will show a sharp, decisive convergence to zero, proving a clean, low-dimensional mode destabilization.
