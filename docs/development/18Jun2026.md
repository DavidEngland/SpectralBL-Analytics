The question of whether the activation energy of the second-derivative dominant mode ($\eta_3$) scales linearly or exponentially as it approaches the fold point ($\gamma \to \gamma_c$) gets to the heart of **critical slowing down (CSD)** in dynamical systems.

If your p-FEM SVD pipeline is capturing a true subcritical saddle-node or Hopf bifurcation, $\eta_3$ should not scale linearly. Because the system is approaching a geometric singularity where the leading eigenvalue $\lambda_{\max}(\mathbf{J}) \to 0$, the math dictates a non-linear, power-law divergence.

## 📈 The Scaling Law of the Fold

As the control parameter $\gamma$ approaches the critical threshold $\gamma_c$, the recovery rate of the system back to its steady-state attractor core slows to zero. In physical space, the atmosphere loses its capacity to damp out vertical profile perturbations.

```
       η3 Amplitude / Variance
          ▲
          │               🛑 Geometric Singularity (\lambda_max = 0)
          │              .│
          │             . │  ◄── Power-law / Exponential-like divergence
          │            .  │      (Structural Critical Slowing Down)
          │          .    │
          │     ..─´      │
          └───´───────────┴────────►
             Far        Near    \gamma \to \gamma_c

```

1. **The Critical Slowing Down Signature:** As $\lambda_{\max}$ approaches 0, the variance and auto-correlation of the structural shape coordinate ($\eta_3$) should experience an **exponential-like power-law divergence** proportional to $(\gamma - \gamma_c)^{-1/2}$ or similar critical exponents.
2. **Why $\eta_3$ Lights Up First:** Because $\eta_3$ measures the vertical profile *curvature* ($\partial^2 Ri / \partial z^2$), it acts as the geometric transducer of the manifold's bending. Bulk energy modes ($\eta_1, \eta_2$) track the massive, sluggish scales of the inversion, but the higher-order geometric features encoded in $\eta_3$ are highly agile—they register the structural "crimping" of the phase space long before the bulk variables drop off the edge.
