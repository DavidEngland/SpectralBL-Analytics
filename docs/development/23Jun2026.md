Atmospheric scientists rarely become excited by eigenvectors themselves. They become interested when an eigenvector can be interpreted as a competing physical process.

(\eta_1,\eta_2,\eta_3)

\mathcal{M}
=

\text{Radiative Cooling}
\;\oplus\;
\text{Shear Production}
\;\oplus\;
\text{Turbulent Mixing}.

Then a trajectory moving across the manifold is no longer an abstract state-space path—it becomes a migration between dominant physical balances.

This framing immediately connects to decades of SBL literature because the weakly stable and very stable regimes can be interpreted as different regions of the same balance manifold rather than separate parameterization categories.

U \rightarrow Ri_g

Ri_g \rightarrow K_z,

\mathcal{F}(\eta_1,\eta_2,\eta_3)=0.

* weak turbulence,
* strong stratification,
* intermittent transport.
